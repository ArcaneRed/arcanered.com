Accessibility Auditing Service

We will evaluate your website or digital service by conducting both automated and manual user testing. Once evaluated we produce an audit report on how well this supports the needs of disabled users. This will enable you to work with actionable items ensuring your website or digital service is compliant against WCAG 2.1.

Our accessibility audits follow a standard format allowing us to offer packages, we can also create a bespoke service where needed. Web accessibility audits can come in all shapes and sizes, we will happily work with you and your team to determine the best option for you.

We have three packages to give you a guide of how much an audit costs and would be more than happy to tailor any package to best suit your needs.

Cost of an audit

Starter audit

Ideal for small websites to ensure it is fully compliant.

  • 10 basic views and 1 moderate view.
  • Automated reporting software and manual testing using various assistive technologies and sequential keyboard navigation.
  • Testing using optimum combination of device, operating system and screen reader.
  • WCAG 2.1 audit report containing summary of findings, scope of the evaluation, audit results detailing the outcome with a reference to the WCAG criteria.

Total cost £1,499

Standard audit

Ideal for small to medium sites and can benefit from additional follow up support.

  • 20 basic views and 4 moderate views and 1 complex view.
  • Automated reporting software and manual testing using various assistive technologies and sequential keyboard navigation.
  • Testing using optimum combination of device, operating system and screen reader.
  • WCAG 2.1 audit report containing summary of findings, scope of the evaluation, audit results detailing the outcome with a reference to the WCAG criteria.
  • Report debrief (1 hour telephone).
  • 2 hours remote support.

Total cost £3,699

Plus audit

Medium to large sites with the additional benefit of support and training.

  • 30 basic views and 10 moderate views and 5 complex views.
  • Automated reporting software and manual testing using various assistive technologies and sequential keyboard navigation.
  • Testing using optimum combination of device, operating system and screen reader.
  • WCAG 2.1 audit report containing summary of findings, scope of the evaluation, audit results detailing the outcome with a reference to the WCAG criteria.
  • Report debrief (1 hour telephone).
  • 4 hours remote support.
  • Remote workshop based on audit results and key areas of support.
  • Slide deck with executive summary of issues.

Total cost £6,999

*Definition of basic, moderate and complex views

A basic view is a page with no interactive elements other than links and the main menu. A moderate view may have a simple form. A complex view may have more complex forms and scripted interactions.
